Bright Motors Car Sales Case Study
Overview of the case study
This case study was completed for Bright Motors to help the new Head of Sales understand sales performance, regional opportunities, pricing patterns, and customer demand trends. The goal was to turn the historical car sales dataset into practical business insights that could support better inventory planning, pricing decisions, and commercial strategy.
The analysis was based on the Bright Motors raw sales data together with the case study brief provided by BrightLearn.
How the case study was done and what insights were found
The case study started with reviewing the project brief and profiling the raw dataset to understand its structure, quality issues, and missing fields. The source data was then cleaned by standardizing text values, parsing dates, handling missing values, and creating derived fields such as sales period, vehicle age, total revenue, sales region, odometer bands, price bands, and a pricing benchmark gap using MMR.
The data cleaning itself was done in Python. After that, the same transformation logic was mirrored in Databricks SQL so the dataset could also be queried for commercial insights in a database environment. The analysis was then presented through Excel visuals, charts, summary tables, PowerPoint slides, Miro planning content, Mermaid and Canva Gantt charts, and a Lovable-ready dashboard prompt for a web-based visual alternative.
The main insights found were:
•	Ford was the highest revenue-generating make.
•	The Ford F-150 was the top revenue-generating model.
•	Florida and California were the strongest-performing states by revenue.
•	Sedans had the highest transaction volume, while SUVs and truck-style vehicles showed stronger selling-value potential.
•	Selling price decreased as mileage increased.
•	Newer vehicles generally achieved higher selling prices than older vehicles.
Summary of what was done and what was found
This case study covered the full analytics workflow from raw data review to final business presentation. The raw sales file was cleaned and transformed into an analysis-ready dataset, SQL scripts were written for Databricks, and multiple reporting outputs were created for presentation and decision-making.
The findings showed clear revenue leaders by make, model, and region, along with useful pricing and demand patterns. The results suggest that Bright Motors should prioritize strong-performing brands in high-revenue regions, improve pricing discipline using vehicle age and mileage, and strengthen future reporting by capturing additional fields such as fuel type, cost price, branch, and customer segment.
